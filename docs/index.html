Algo Strategies

Strategies Under Governance · Synthetic-First Laboratories

Algo Strategies is a governance-first pillar for implementing, stress-testing, and explaining canonical strategy archetypes in a controlled, synthetic environment. It is deliberately explicit about what it is not: it does not promise alpha discovery, market prediction, or a shortcut to deployable production systems. Its purpose is more demanding and more useful: mechanism clarity under constraint. If you cannot explain why a policy behaves the way it behaves when the market state changes, then performance is just a story you tell yourself while the risk accumulates off-screen.

The decision to use synthetic markets is not an aesthetic choice. It is a governance choice. Real data seduces practitioners into narrative reasoning: “it worked before, so it works.” Synthetic construction forces a different posture: you must state your assumptions, encode them, and accept that the environment will expose the strategy’s dependencies. In this pillar, regimes are not hand-waved; they are parameterized. Liquidity is not a footnote; it is a binding constraint. Costs are not a line item; they are a surface that reshapes the feasible action space. The goal is not to mimic history. The goal is to isolate the structural channels—trend persistence, mean reversion, carry, factor dispersion, correlation compression, execution convexity—and watch how they interact with the policy you claim to understand.

Most “strategy education” fails in predictable ways. It confuses narrative with mechanism, treats costs as rounding error, and turns backtests into mythology. A backtest can be a useful diagnostic, but it is not evidence of understanding. It is a single sample path under a particular cost model, a particular rebalance cadence, and a particular regime sequence. This pillar refuses that bargain. Every notebook implements a full governed pipeline: synthetic market construction → signal isolation → portfolio rules → costs/slippage → stress tests → stage gates → artifact logging. The point is not to optimize. The point is to remove hiding places. If a strategy only looks “good” when you ignore turnover, dilute impact, assume perfect fills, or pretend regimes don’t break, the laboratory is designed to make that obvious quickly and reproducibly.

The core idea is simple: strategies are policies operating inside constrained control systems. A signal may be clever, but realized outcomes are shaped by feasibility and control: execution, turnover limits, liquidity floors, leverage caps, volatility targeting, drawdown stops, and the geometry of correlation under stress. These are not implementation details; they are the real strategy. Governance is therefore not decoration; it is the condition for honest research. Each run produces a disciplined audit trail—manifests, risk logs, and deliverables—explicitly marked Not verified until independently reviewed. That posture is the entire point: a professional system is not defined by how confidently it speaks, but by how well it can be reconstructed, challenged, and supervised.

Explicit positioning: This pillar teaches strategy archetypes as governed mechanisms. It is not an alpha-seeking program. Reproducibility and auditability dominate optimization.

The Book

The book is the conceptual spine of the pillar. The notebooks are not substitutes for the book, and the book is not complete without execution. Together they form a single, governed apprenticeship unit: mechanism → implementation → stress → gate decision → artifacts.

Book — Strategy for Traders

A governance-first framing of strategy archetypes: how to implement mechanisms in synthetic markets, isolate signals, impose portfolio constraints, model costs, stress the system, and produce auditable deliverables. The emphasis is structural honesty rather than performance storytelling.

Operating rule: Interpret notebook outcomes as governed experimental outputs. They are tagged “Not verified” by design until independently reviewed and replicated.

This pillar is unusually strict about research hygiene.
